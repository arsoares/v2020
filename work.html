Work

Chewy for iOS

When I started at Chewy, we had 4-5 designers work across web and native to bridge our web experience to the app. My role was to synthesize and standardize all of the redesign work that was started to a native design for Chewy, including the upcoming iPad platform.


Learn about Universal App

Standards, Libraries, and Focus

Some of my earliest tasks were to make allies with our exceptional development team to standardize fonts, cut down on extra components and one-off styles, and build consistency even if it made us return and edit approved work. I work in systems and built the first iOS design system in Sketch to do this.

Responsive Design ≠ Native Design

Typically with responsive design, I am used to setting different font sizes, spacing, and margins across phone, tablet, and desktop. With a universal app, these standard values are instead shared across iPhone and iPad. We designed in a way that allowed us to stay true to the system and be able to show more content if possible from iPhone in portrait to the iPad Pro 12.9 in landscape.


Embracing the Human Interface Guidelines

When the iPhone X was released before my arrival, it brought with it new gestures and considerations like safe areas and elongated navigation. We crafted our experience to respect the HIG, then took it a step further with our deep focus on creating a fully-accessible app by supporting VoiceOver and later in iOS 13, Voice Control. We continue to improve our app with each release.
